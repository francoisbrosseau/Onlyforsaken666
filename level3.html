The Eye That Follow

The corridor narrows like a throat. The air tastes of cold iron and old prayers. Somewhere behind the stone, something drags its nails across a memory it does not own.

You pass a blackened statue—faceless, yet watching. Its shadow does not match the torchlight. The deeper you walk, the more you feel it: a gaze that does not blink.

The castle offers no directions. Only hints… if you dare to look where you shouldn’t.
