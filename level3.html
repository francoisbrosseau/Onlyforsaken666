The Silent Watcher

Level 3

The door did not open.

It unfolded.

Stone pulled away from stone without sound, as if the castle feared to wake something older than itself. The air beyond was warmer, but it carried the smell of dust… and breath.

Not yours.

You step inside.

The gate seals behind you without force, without echo, without mercy.

Ahead stretches a corridor swallowed by shadow. No torches. No windows. Yet you feel seen.

Halfway down the hall, you notice it.

A figure carved into the wall — tall, robed, faceless. Its head tilts ever so slightly, though you would swear it is stone.

Watching.

Waiting.

Judging.

At its feet lies a narrow pedestal, and on it, words carved shallow, almost erased by time:

“I have no mouth, yet I ask.
I have no eyes, yet I witness.
Give me what I seek,
and the path shall live.”

The Eye That Follows

The statue does not move.
Yet when you shift your attention… it feels closer.

Touch nothing. Hover, and listen.
